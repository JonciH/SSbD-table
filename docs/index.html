SSbD Project Tracker

Following the last SSbD meeting in Brussels by the European Commission, there was a clear call for stronger coordination across RTD activities. In response, I've created a sortable, filterable reference table of EU Horizon-funded projects working within the Safe and Sustainable by Design (SSbD) framework, inspired by Fantke, P. Sustain. Chem. Pharm. 2025, 45, 101986.

Designed for SSbD practitioners, Horizon consortium partners, industrial stakeholders, and European Commission project officers, the table provides a single, searchable overview of who is working on what in the SSbD landscape.

— Jonas Hoffmann, GreenDelta, Sustainability Researcher

About this table. All project data — acronyms, full titles, coordinators, project websites, funding call/topic IDs, start/end dates, coordinator countries, and budget figures — is sourced from CORDIS, the European Commission's R&I results database. Partner-role columns (technology development, risk assessment, environmental/social LCA, AI, MCDA scoring, industrial use cases, dissemination, exploitation) are not tagged in CORDIS by role and are filled in through manual expert review — these are the fields most likely to need a community correction. The SSbD core project flag marks projects where the SSbD framework was central to the project's rationale — either because "Safe and Sustainable by Design" (or its H2020 predecessor "Safe by Design") appeared explicitly in the funding call title, or because the project was part of the EU's established SSbD ecosystem (e.g. PARC, NANORIGO, PARASOL, SAFETYFANS) even if the call predates or does not use the exact SSbD terminology. Projects marked No apply SSbD as a design methodology but were funded under broader calls (e.g. bio-based innovation, circular bioeconomy, battery recycling, hydrogen) where SSbD was not the primary funding rationale.
